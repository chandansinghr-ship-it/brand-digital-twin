We're building the system that shows what your campaigns *actually* return —
after COGS, shipping, refunds, discounts, and the attribution double-counting
your dashboards quietly do.

We're opening it to **3 founding brands**. Free. Hands-on. You help us find
where it breaks; we hand you a profit teardown most agencies charge five figures for.

**[ Apply to the Founding Cohort ]**  ·  *3 spots · early access · no card*

> Sub-hook (smaller): *Not a trial. A working partnership with the team building it.*

**Conversion job:** filter in the operator who already feels the ROAS/margin gap;
filter out the tire-kicker who wants a free dashboard. The word "apply" does the work.

## STAGE 2 — RECOGNISE (the problem, in their words)

### You've felt this even if no tool ever showed it to you.

- A top campaign quietly running traffic to an out-of-stock product.
- "Profitable" ROAS that evaporates once shipping and returns land.
- Three platforms each claiming the same sale.
- A month that looked great on the dashboard and thin in the account.

None of your dashboards flagged it — none were built to. They report
*performance*. None of them report *profit*.

**Conversion job:** earn the nod. They should think "this is my Tuesday."
Recognition, not persuasion.

---

## STAGE 3 — TRUST (what's real today — radical honesty as proof)

### Here's exactly what works right now — and what doesn't yet.

We'd rather show you the seams than oversell. This is what's **live and verified
in the codebase today:**

**Working now:**
- **POAS truth engine** — real profit per campaign: COGS, fulfilment, refunds,
  discounts, last-touch attribution.
- **ROAS and POAS side by side** — the gap, on one screen.
- **Diagnostic sweep** — five live checks: stockout-on-promoted-SKU, spend with
  no conversion tracking, broken checkout events, unprofitable spend,
  budget-capped winners — ranked by dollars at stake.
- **Healing cards** — every problem comes with *why*, *what we can fix*, and an
  honest *what ads can't fix* line.
- **Cold-start** — brand new with a catalog but no sales? We score margin from
  your catalog and tell you which products can carry paid acquisition.
- **Graduated autonomy** — you decide, per change, how much it acts alone. Undo anything.

**Honest about what's not there yet (you'd help us build it):**
- Direct bank connections (RBI Account Aggregator / Plaid) — in progress.
- Owned-channel depth (email, organic, reviews) — Phase 2.
- Incrementality holdout testing — we flag suspect campaigns, don't yet prove lift.

**Conversion job:** the honesty *is* the credibility. An operator who's been burned
by overclaiming SaaS trusts the team that names its own gaps. This converts the skeptic.

## STAGE 4 — SELF-QUALIFY (the 3 profiles see themselves)

### We're looking for three specific kinds of brand.

Pick the one that's you — it tells us where to point the teardown.

**🔴 The paid-heavy operator**
$20K–$80K/mo ad spend, Shopify, messy or partial COGS. You live in the ad
accounts and suspect the profit picture is worse than the dashboard. *We test the
core POAS + healing loop on you.*

**🟡 The early brand**
Catalog's live, under ~50 orders, little ad history. You want to know which
products can support paid before you burn budget finding out. *We test cold-start
and getting you to a trustworthy number from near-zero.*

**🟢 The organic-led brand**
Real organic and email traffic, modest paid. You want to know what's actually
driving sales — and where ads genuinely can't help. *We test the cross-channel
view and the honest "ads can't fix this" boundary.*

> We're taking **one of each** to start. India or global both welcome — we run on
> both markets' rails (Tally/RBI AA and QuickBooks/Plaid; WhatsApp and Slack).

**Conversion job:** turn a generic visitor into a specific applicant. Self-selection
raises application quality and gives you the contrast cohort the validation plan needs.

## STAGE 5 — THE EXCHANGE (what you give / what you get)

### A real partnership, stated plainly.

**You get:**
- Full access, free, for the whole cohort period — no card, no cutoff.
- A hands-on profit teardown of your actual account, with the team.
- Direct line to the people building it; your stalls become the roadmap.
- Founding-partner pricing locked in when we open paid plans.

**We ask:**
- ~3 weeks of real usage on real (messy is fine — preferred) data.
- Three short conversations: a baseline interview, a mid-point check, an exit interview.
- Permission to watch where you stall — honestly, so we fix it.
- Candour. "I didn't act on this card because…" is the most valuable thing you can give us.

**Conversion job:** make the ask concrete and bounded so "apply" feels low-risk and
high-return. The reciprocity (teardown for feedback) is the trade that closes.

## STAGE 6 — APPLY (the conversion event)

### Tell us about your brand. Two minutes.

A short form — not a signup wall:

1. Brand name + website
2. Which profile fits (paid-heavy / early / organic-led)
3. Rough monthly ad spend
4. Platforms you'd connect (Shopify, Google Ads, Meta, GA4…)
5. The one number you wish you trusted but don't
6. Email

**[ Submit Application ]**

> No credit card. No auto-trial. A human reads every one and replies within 2 days.

**Conversion job:** Q5 ("the one number you wish you trusted") is the qualifier in
disguise — it surfaces intent and gives the teardown its opening hook. Keep the form
short; selectivity comes from the reply, not the form length.

---

## STAGE 7 — ONBOARD (what happens after apply — close the loop)

### If we're a fit, here's the first week.

1. **We reply** (≤2 days) — yes/not-yet, and a 20-min call to confirm fit.
2. **Goal-first setup** — you declare your primary goal; we light up only the
   integrations that goal needs (see `USER_JOURNEYS.md`).
3. **Baseline scan + connect** — the sweep runs; you see your first findings ranked by dollars.
4. **Your teardown call** — we walk your real POAS and healing cards together.
5. **Three weeks, your pace** — minimal hand-holding; we watch where it stalls.

**Conversion job:** removing post-apply uncertainty reduces drop-off between
"applied" and "actually onboarded" — the gap where pilots usually die.

## NOTES FOR DESIGN / DEV

**Visual direction**
- Hero: the dual-metric card (ROAS 4.2× vs POAS 0.8×) — same asset as public LP, but
  with a "3 spots" counter chip beside the CTA.
- Stage 3 "what's real / what's not" = a two-column ledger (✓ live / ◷ building).
  This honesty block is the page's signature — design it to feel confident, not apologetic.
- Stage 4 profiles = three cards, colour-coded (red/amber/green) matching the journey docs.
- Stage 6 form = single screen, inline, no redirect. Q5 is a free-text field given visual weight.

**Tone**
- Same BAU voice as the public LP — operator-to-operator, no manifesto.
- The difference is *posture*: this page is invitational and selective, not promotional.
- Never say "limited time" — say "3 spots." Real scarcity, not urgency theatre.

**Instrumentation (this page feeds the validation plan)**
- Track which profile card drives applications — tells us where demand concentrates.
- Capture Q5 answers verbatim — qualitative gold for messaging the public LP later.
- Funnel metric that matters: not application *volume*, but **right-fit applications per profile**.
  Three good ones beats three hundred wrong ones.

**Where it lives**
- Standalone page (e.g. `/founding` or a Typeform-class form behind a one-pager).
- Not linked from any future public nav. Shared directly into founder/DTC communities,
  per the validation plan's recruit channels.
