# Brand Baseline Scan — Observable Reality Before Connection

> Stage 0 of the journey. Runs on nothing but a brand name + domain, before any
> account is connected. Establishes where the brand stands across its full
> digital footprint — and becomes the context layer every healing recommendation
> consults before it prescribes.
>
> Companion: `HEALING_RECOMMENDATIONS.md`, `PROFIT_DATA_MODEL.md`, `USER_JOURNEYS.md`.

---

## The Core Distinction: Observable vs. Owned

There are two kinds of data, and only one needs the user.

| | **Observable** | **Owned** |
|--|---------------|-----------|
| Examples | SERP rankings, review sentiment, ad presence, social footprint, site health, share of voice | Orders, ad spend, COGS, email metrics, true conversion |
| Requires | Nothing — public data | OAuth, connection, effort |
| Tells you | Where the brand *stands* (presence, perception, trust) | What the brand *earns* (economics, profit) |
| Available | Before any connection | After connection |

The baseline scan lives almost entirely in the **Observable** layer. This is
what makes it possible to deliver a 360° context picture — and first value —
*before* the connection wall.

---

## What the Scan Establishes (zero user effort)

From a brand name + domain alone:

| Dimension | Observable signals | Public source |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Presence** | Organic rankings, indexed page count, branded search volume, site speed / Core Web Vitals, mobile usability | SERP, GSC public surfaces, PageSpeed Insights |
| **Paid footprint** | Active ad creatives, channels in use, rough spend posture, messaging angles | Meta Ad Library, Google Ads Transparency Center |
| **Perception** | Review volume, average rating, rating trend over time, sentiment polarity | Google Business Profile, Trustpilot, app stores, marketplace listings |
| **Trust / reputation** | Brand mentions, press coverage, share of voice vs. named competitors, unanswered complaints | Social, news, review platforms |
| **Social standing** | Follower base, engagement rate, posting cadence, content mix | Public Instagram / Facebook / LinkedIn / X profiles |
| **Catalog visibility** | Shopping presence, product feed coverage, price competitiveness | Google Shopping, marketplace search |

This is a near-complete picture of *where a brand stands* — assembled before
they lift a finger.

---

## The Boundary: Presence ≠ Economics

The scan **contextualises** the prescription. It does not **complete** it.

It can tell you a brand ranks #1 organically and holds a 4.6 rating. It cannot
tell you the COGS, the true conversion rate, or the real POAS. Its job is precise
and limited: supply the cross-channel awareness that makes advice *safe*, without
pretending to see the economics it can't.

Example of the scan doing its job inside a healing card:
> *"Shift budget off Summer Jackets — but note you rank #2 organically for this
>  product. Paid may be defending the SERP against a competitor. Verify before
>  pausing."*

The scan supplied the organic-rank context. The owned data (POAS) supplied the
economics. Together they produce safe advice; neither alone could.

---

## The Discipline Guardrail

Perception and trust scanning is exactly where vanity metrics breed — the very
thing the OS exists to reject. The rule that prevents rot:

> **Every scanned signal must change a recommendation, or it doesn't get shown.**

A sentiment score that sits on a dashboard is the suite trap wearing a halo.
A sentiment drop that *downgrades the confidence of a "scale this" recommendation*
is intelligence. Same data, opposite value. The scan feeds the decision spine
or it does not exist.

---

## Why the Scan Earns Its Place — Four Problems, One Move

1. **Blind spots** — advice accounts for channels the user hasn't connected yet
2. **Cold-start** — works for a brand-new business with zero owned data
3. **Low-friction onboarding** — first value *before* any OAuth
4. **Connection driver** — *"You rank #1 for 'organic protein bars' — connect
   Search Console so we don't tell you to bid against your own free traffic."*
   The gap drives the connection at the moment it matters, not at a wall.

## The Improvement Layer — Suggestions to Raise the Observable Baseline

The scan is not just a mirror. For every observable weakness it finds, it pairs
a concrete, mostly-free improvement. These are presence/perception fixes the
brand can act on *without* connecting anything — building goodwill before the
paid relationship even begins.

### Presence

| Observed weakness | Suggested improvement |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Slow site / poor Core Web Vitals | Specific pages to fix; image compression, lazy-load, CDN — quantified speed gain and its conversion impact |
| Thin organic footprint / few indexed pages | Content gaps vs. competitors; the high-margin SKUs with no ranking content |
| Branded search exists but no branded landing pages | Capture branded intent before competitors bid on it |
| No structured data / schema | Add product/review schema for rich results — free SERP real estate |

### Paid footprint

| Observed weakness | Suggested improvement |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Competitors advertising on your brand terms | Defend with a low-cost brand campaign before they erode it |
| Stale or single creative angle in ad library | Creative angles competitors use that you don't |
| Absent from a channel where the audience lives | Channel whitespace where competitors have no presence |

### Perception & trust

| Observed weakness | Suggested improvement |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Low review volume | Post-purchase review request flow — the single highest-leverage trust move |
| Declining rating trend | The themes driving recent negatives (sizing, shipping, quality) — fix the source |
| Unanswered negative reviews | Respond to the most-visible ones; response itself lifts perception |
| Inconsistent NAP / no Google Business presence | Claim and complete the profile — foundational local trust |

### Social standing

| Observed weakness | Suggested improvement |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Low engagement despite followers | Content format mix that earns engagement in the category |
| Irregular posting | Cadence baseline vs. category leaders |
| No social proof surfaced on site | Pull UGC / reviews onto product pages — converts the traffic you already have |

**The framing:** these are not upsells. They are the brand's own
reality reflected back with a path to improve it — the same WHAT → WHY → FIX
logic as the healing engine, applied to presence instead of profit. They build
trust in the OS before a single dollar of ad spend is touched.

## How the Scan Feeds the Healing Engine

The baseline becomes a persistent **context layer** the healing engine consults
before every prescription:

- **Organic rank context** → guards "pause paid" advice on terms the brand ranks for
- **Competitor presence** → flags when pausing cedes ground to a named competitor
- **Perception trend** → downgrades confidence on "scale" advice when rating is falling
- **Channel whitespace** → informs where to expand, not just where to cut

Each recommendation carries its own completeness honesty:
> *"Based on paid + commerce data and your observable footprint. Email and
>  organic not connected — connect them so this accounts for cross-channel
>  effects."*

This resolves the roadmap tension cleanly: cross-channel *ingestion* need not
move into Phase 1 — the scan pulls each channel's *observable shadow* in for
free, and the gaps drive connection over time.
